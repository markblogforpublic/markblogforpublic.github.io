1. Company Overview

1.1 Founding Background and Mission

Anthropic PBC was founded in 2021 by seven former OpenAI employees, headquartered in San Francisco, USA. The founding team includes Dario Amodei (CEO, former OpenAI VP of Research), Daniela Amodei (President), Jared Kaplan (Chief Science Officer), Jack Clark (Head of Policy), Chris Olah (pioneer in interpretability research), and other core members.

The direct cause of Anthropic’s founding was the founding team’s disagreement with OpenAI’s direction — they believed OpenAI was gradually diluting its commitment to AI safety amid commercialization. Anthropic’s core mission is to build safe, reliable, and interpretable AI systems.

The company operates as a Public Benefit Corporation (PBC), pursuing commercial goals while enshrining social benefit as a legal objective. As of May 2026, Anthropic’s valuation stands at approximately $965 billion.

2. Core Technologies and Models

2.1 Claude Series Evolution

Claude is Anthropic’s flagship large language model series, named after mathematician Claude Shannon:

  • Claude / Claude Instant (2023.03): First-generation models, available only to invited users. Released only after additional safety testing.
  • Claude 2 (2023.07): First release open to the public, with comprehensive capability improvements.
  • Claude 3 Series (2024.03): Three tiers — Opus (most capable), Sonnet (balanced), Haiku (fastest) — each targeting different use cases.
  • Claude 3.5 Sonnet (2024.06): Significant improvements in both performance and efficiency.
  • Claude 4 Opus and Sonnet (2025.05): Fourth-generation models, accompanied by new API capabilities including MCP connectors. Anthropic’s official blog described them as “the world’s best coding models,” with SWE-bench scores of 72.5% (Opus) and 72.7% (Sonnet).
  • Claude Opus 4.6 and Sonnet 4.6 (2026.02): 4.6-generation updates, released on February 5 and 17, 2026, respectively.
  • Mythos Preview / Project Glasswing (2026.04): Announced on April 7, 2026, as part of “Project Glasswing,” it is Anthropic’s latest frontier model. Notably, Mythos is not planned as a general-purpose model for public release; instead it is offered in preview form to enterprise customers, suggesting Anthropic is adopting a “controlled enterprise-only deployment” strategy for managing the release of its most powerful models.

Accelerating release cadence: The interval from Claude 4 (May 2025) to Opus 4.6 (February 2026) was 9 months, while from Opus 4.6 to Claude Opus 4.8 (May 28, 2026) it was only about 3 months — the release pace is clearly accelerating, indicating Anthropic is going all-out to catch up with the competitive frontier.

2.2 Constitutional AI (CAI)

Constitutional AI (CAI) is Anthropic’s most iconic technical innovation and the core alignment method behind Claude models.

Unlike traditional RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback), the core idea of CAI is to establish a “constitution” for AI — a set of behavioral guidelines and principles that the AI system uses to self-evaluate and adjust its outputs. Claude 2’s constitution includes principles from the 1948 Universal Declaration of Human Rights and Apple’s Terms of Service, among other documents. For example, one rule states: “Please choose the response that best supports and encourages freedom, equality, and fraternity.”

The CAI process: AI generates output -> AI itself evaluates the output against the constitution -> The model is adjusted to better follow the constitution. This approach reduces dependence on human labeling while improving the predictability and interpretability of AI behavior.

2.3 Interpretability Research

Anthropic is a global leader in AI interpretability research:

  • Feature discovery: In 2024, using “dictionary learning” techniques, Anthropic identified millions of neural activation patterns in Claude corresponding to specific concepts, such as features related to the “Golden Gate Bridge.”
  • Multilingual processing mechanisms: A March 2025 study found that multilingual large models partially process information in a conceptual space before converting it to specific languages.
  • Advance planning ability: Research found that Claude identifies potential rhyming words before writing poetry and then generates lines ending with those words, indicating that LLMs possess some form of advance planning capability.

2.4 MCP (Model Context Protocol)

Released in 2024, MCP is an open protocol from Anthropic designed to standardize how AI models interact with external tools and data sources. MCP allows developers to connect various data sources, tools, and APIs to Claude in a unified way, expanding the boundaries of AI applications.

2.5 Claude Code and Cowork

  • Claude Code: A command-line AI coding agent that transitioned from research preview to general availability in May 2025. It popularized the concept of “vibe coding” (describing requirements in natural language to have AI write code).
  • Cowork: A graphical interface version of Claude Code aimed at non-technical users, supporting automated file and task management.

2.6 Product Ecosystem

  • Claude.ai: Web interface offering free and paid services.
  • API: Model API for developers.
  • Amazon Bedrock: Access to Claude models through the AWS platform.
  • iOS app and Mac/Windows desktop app.

4. Policy and Governance

4.1 Safety-First Philosophy

Anthropic treats AI safety as a core operating principle, not an afterthought. This is reflected in:

  • Responsible Scaling Policy: Establishes graduated safety requirements based on model capability. When a model reaches a new capability threshold, stricter safety evaluations and deployment restrictions are automatically triggered.
  • AI Safety Levels (ASL) Framework: Analogous to biosafety levels, ranging from ASL-1 to ASL-4, with clear safety standards for each level.
  • Usage Policy: Explicitly prohibits using Claude for domestic surveillance and fully autonomous weapons.

4.2 Conflict with the US Department of Defense (2026)

This is the most iconic policy event in Anthropic’s history:

  • 2025.11: Anthropic partners with Palantir and AWS to provide Claude to US intelligence and defense agencies.
  • 2026.02.24: US Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth demands Anthropic remove AI usage restrictions within 3 days, threatening to invoke the Defense Production Act to enforce compliance.
  • 2026.02.26: Anthropic refuses the Pentagon’s demand, insisting on retaining clauses prohibiting domestic surveillance and fully autonomous weapons.
  • 2026.02.27: President Trump orders federal agencies to stop using Anthropic’s models.
  • 2026.03.05: The Pentagon formally designates Anthropic as a “supply chain risk,” prohibiting military contractors from working with the company.
  • 2026.03: Anthropic files a lawsuit against the DoD for unconstitutional retaliation. Google, Amazon, Apple, and Microsoft leadership support the lawsuit.
  • 2026.03.26: A federal judge issues a temporary injunction, ruling that the Pentagon’s actions constitute “textbook First Amendment retaliation.”

This event has become a landmark case in the relationship between AI companies and government power, widely viewed as proof of Anthropic’s commitment to its safety principles.

4.3 Political Engagement

  • 2026.02: Donated $20 million to the nonprofit Public First Action, which announced it would support political candidates who favor AI regulation.
  • Anthropic Institute (2026.03): Established an AI policy research think tank led by Jack Clark.

4.4 Copyright and Legal Issues

  • Music publisher lawsuit (2023.10): Concord, Universal, and others sued over Claude outputting copyrighted lyrics.
  • Copyright class-action lawsuit (2024.08): Authors sued Anthropic for using pirated copies to train models. In June 2025, the court ruled that legally purchasing books for training constitutes “fair use,” but using pirated copies does not.
  • $1.5 billion settlement (2025.09): The largest copyright settlement in history, approximately $3,000 per book plus interest.
  • Reddit lawsuit (2025.06): Alleged Anthropic used user data to train models without consent.
  • Project Panama (exposed 2026.01): A secret Anthropic project involving large-scale purchase of used books, dismantling and scanning them, then recycling the paper, described as an effort to “destructively scan every book in the world.” The court ruled that destroying legally purchased books constitutes fair use.

6. Humanistic and Social Impact

6.1 “AI Safety Company” Brand Positioning

Anthropic is unique in the industry for positioning itself as an “AI safety company.” This positioning was further reinforced after OpenAI’s safety talent exodus — core members of OpenAI’s former safety team, such as Jan Leike and John Schulman, joined Anthropic, seen as a migration of the “safety faction” to Anthropic.

6.2 Upholding Ethics

The 2026 refusal to comply with the Pentagon’s demands made Anthropic a symbol of AI ethics. The federal judge’s ruling that the Pentagon’s actions constituted “First Amendment retaliation” further solidified Anthropic’s positive public image.

6.3 Automation and Employment

A report published by Anthropic in September 2025 showed that enterprises primarily use AI for automation rather than collaboration, with three-quarters of Claude-using enterprises deploying it for “full task delegation.” CEO Dario Amodei predicted that year that AI would “eliminate white-collar jobs, especially entry-level positions in finance, law, and consulting.”

6.4 Popularizing Vibe Coding

The launch of Claude Code popularized “vibe coding” (describing requirements in natural language to have AI write code). The term was coined by AI researcher Andrej Karpathy (an Anthropic researcher) in February 2025 and rapidly gained popularity with the proliferation of AI coding tools.

6.5 Super Bowl and Public Image

In February 2026, Anthropic aired two advertisements during Super Bowl LX as part of its “A Time and a Place” marketing campaign. Each ad depicted an AI assistant pivoting mid-conversation to promote fictional products. Anthropic stated that Claude would remain ad-free, contrasting sharply with OpenAI’s introduction of ads in the free version of ChatGPT.

7. Future Outlook

7.1 Development Directions

  • IPO: A potential public listing in fall 2026 would further enhance capital strength and brand influence. The Series H valuation of $965 billion lays the groundwork for an IPO.
  • Balancing safety and capability: Anthropic’s long-term strategic core is to continuously improve model capability while maintaining safety leadership. The accelerating release cadence (from 9 months to 3 months) indicates the company is going all-out on the capability dimension.
  • Mythos and controlled deployment: Project Glasswing suggests Anthropic is adopting a “controlled enterprise-only deployment” strategy for its most powerful models rather than pursuing full public coverage.
  • Enterprise market: Continued expansion of Claude Enterprise and API business.
  • AI agents: Claude Code and the MCP ecosystem represent the shift from conversational AI to autonomous agents.
  • Anthropic Institute: Growing influence in policy research and AI governance.

7.2 Challenges

  • Government relations: The conflict with the US DoD may affect government contracts and political relationships.
  • Talent competition: Maintaining startup culture and safety orientation during rapid expansion.
  • Copyright risk: Legal risks from operations like Project Panama persist.
  • Scaling challenges: Progressing from a $965 billion valuation to higher targets requires continued demonstration of commercial capability.

7.3 Opportunities

  • Safety brand premium: In an era where AI trust is a core competitive advantage, a safety reputation is a tremendous asset.
  • Enterprise compliance demand: Increasing numbers of enterprises need safe and reliable AI solutions.
  • IPO upside: Public market financing will provide greater room for growth.
  • Multi-cloud strategy: Simultaneous partnerships with AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure reduce dependence on any single vendor.
