DeepSeek, Qwen, Kimi Frontier Research Report

Three leading Chinese AI models: MoE architecture, GRPO algorithm, hybrid reasoning, and training efficiency innovations

DeepSeek, Qwen, Kimi Frontier Research Report

Report date: May 30, 2026 Coverage period: December 2024 — July 2025

Abstract

DeepSeek, Qwen (Tongyi Qianwen, Alibaba), and Kimi (Moonshot AI) are currently the three most influential research forces in China’s large AI model landscape. Between 2024 and 2025, they achieved a series of breakthrough developments, with core innovations concentrated in the following directions:

  • Reinforcement learning (RL)-driven emergence of reasoning capabilities: DeepSeek-R1-Zero demonstrated that pure RL training alone can elicit chain-of-thought reasoning capabilities
  • MoE (Mixture-of-Experts) architectural engineering breakthroughs: Training a 671B parameter model at extremely low cost
  • Hybrid reasoning modes: Qwen3 enables free switching between /think and /no_think modes within a single model
  • Long context and long-chain reasoning optimization: Kimi k1.5 proposed long2short technology, distilling long-chain reasoning capabilities into short-chain reasoning models
  • Training efficiency innovations: FP8 mixed-precision training, the Muon optimizer, and other techniques reduced training costs by an order of magnitude

1. DeepSeek

1.1 DeepSeek-V3: Ultra-Efficient MoE Architecture

Release date: December 2024

Architecture highlights:

  • Total parameters: 671B (67.1 billion)
  • Active parameters per token: 37B (approximately 3.7 billion)
  • Architecture type: Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) with fine-grained expert routing

Key technical innovations:

TechnologyDescription
Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA)Compresses KV Cache into a low-rank latent space and dynamically reconstructs it at inference time, reducing KV Cache memory by approximately 93% while maintaining or exceeding standard MHA performance and running faster than GQA
DeepSeekMoEFine-grained expert partitioning + shared expert mechanism, supplemented by an auxiliary-loss-free load balancing strategy
FP8 mixed-precision trainingEmploys FP8 precision in large-scale training, significantly reducing GPU memory usage and computational cost

Training efficiency:

  • Training data: 14.8 trillion tokens
  • Training cost: Approximately $5.5 million (far below the hundreds of millions of dollars typical for comparable closed-source models)
  • Performance comparable to GPT-4o and Claude 3.5 Sonnet

Paper: DeepSeek-V3 Technical Report (arXiv, December 2024)

1.2 DeepSeek-R1: RL-Driven Reasoning Revolution

Release date: January 2025

DeepSeek-R1-Zero: Reasoning Emergence from Pure RL Training

This is one of the most striking AI research findings of 2025:

  • Training method: Uses only reinforcement learning (RL), completely without supervised fine-tuning (SFT) as a preliminary step
  • Core finding: The model spontaneously developed chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning, self-reflection, and self-correction capabilities during RL training
  • “Aha Moment”: During training, the model suddenly began generating longer, more structured reasoning chains, demonstrating emergent behavior of “learning to think”
  • Significance: Challenges the traditional assumption that “SFT is a necessary prerequisite for building reasoning models,” demonstrating that large language models can develop complex problem-solving strategies through purely trial-and-error reinforcement learning

Limitations: R1-Zero suffered from poor readability and language mixing issues, so the final R1 model incorporated a cold-start SFT phase to improve output quality.

DeepSeek-R1 Complete Training Pipeline

Cold-start SFT -> RL (GRPO) -> Rejection sampling SFT -> Second-stage RL

GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimization)

GRPO is DeepSeek’s core RL algorithm innovation:

How it works:

  1. For each prompt, generates a group of responses (e.g., 64 candidates)
  2. Scores each response using a rule-based reward function (e.g., math/code correctness checks)
  3. Rewards are relatively normalized within the group: advantage = (reward - mean(rewards)) / std(rewards)
  4. Updates the policy using PPO-style clipped objectives

Key advantages:

  • Eliminates the critic/value model: Replaces the value function with group-averaged rewards, drastically reducing GPU memory and computational overhead
  • More computationally efficient than standard PPO

2.2 QwQ-32B: Reasoning Model Exploration

Release date: Late 2024 / Early 2025

  • Positioning: A reasoning-type model similar to OpenAI’s o1
  • Parameter scale: 32B
  • Training method: Applies reinforcement learning (RL) on top of a strong pre-trained base model
  • Benchmark performance: On reasoning benchmarks such as AIME 2024 (math competition), LiveCodeBench (code), and GPQA (graduate-level science Q&A), approaches or matches the performance of DeepSeek-R1 and o1-mini
  • Open source: Released with open weights

Technical report statement:

“These findings highlight the effectiveness of applying RL to robust foundation models pre-trained on a vast corpus of world knowledge, and indicate that scaling RL has the potential to elevate model performance beyond what traditional pre-training and post-training methods can achieve.”

Core Innovation: Hybrid Thinking Mode

Qwen3’s most iconic innovation is reasoning mode switching within a single model:

  • /think mode: The model performs extended chain-of-thought reasoning (similar to o1 style), suited for complex math, logic, and coding tasks
  • /no_think mode: The model provides answers directly, suited for fast-response scenarios
  • Thinking budget control: Users can choose when to enable deep reasoning based on task complexity

Significance: A single model serves both reasoning and non-reasoning use cases without the need to deploy different models.

Benchmark Performance

  • The flagship model Qwen3-235B-A22B competes with or surpasses DeepSeek-R1, OpenAI o1, GPT-4o, and Grok-3 across multiple benchmarks
  • Qwen3-30B-A3B (MoE) surpasses QwQ-32B with fewer active parameters
  • Strong performance across mathematical reasoning (AIME, MATH-500), code generation (LiveCodeBench), general knowledge (GPQA), and multilingual tasks (supporting 100+ languages)

Open-Source Contributions

The full Qwen3 series is released with open weights, available on Hugging Face and ModelScope, offering both base and instruction-tuned versions.

3. Kimi (Moonshot AI)

3.1 Long Context Pioneer

Kimi is a pioneer in long context processing in China’s AI field:

  • One of the first commercial models to support a 200K token context window
  • Subsequently expanded the context window to the millions of tokens range
  • Established advantages in long document understanding, multi-turn dialogue retention, and cross-document reasoning
  • Conducted extensive engineering innovations in KV Cache optimization and long context training

3.2 Kimi k1.5: Long-Chain to Short-Chain Distillation for RL Reasoning

Release date: January 2025

Core positioning: Multimodal reasoning model trained with reinforcement learning

Key Technical Contributions

Long2Short (long-chain to short-chain) technology:

  • Distills long-chain reasoning (Long-CoT) capabilities into short-chain reasoning (Short-CoT) models
  • Significantly reduces token consumption and latency at inference time while maintaining strong reasoning capabilities
  • Makes models more efficient in practical deployment

Other technical highlights:

  • Long-CoT to short-CoT distillation
  • Partial rollout strategy in RL training
  • Improved reward modeling
  • Curriculum learning to enhance training efficiency

Multimodal Capabilities

k1.5 is designed to handle multimodal inputs (text and images), enabling cross-modal reasoning.

Benchmark Performance

Achieved competitive results on math, code, and reasoning benchmarks, matching or surpassing leading reasoning models such as OpenAI o1 on multiple tasks.

Paper: Kimi k1.5: Scaling Reinforcement Learning with LLMs (Moonshot AI, January 2025)

Core Innovation: Muon Optimizer

A key training innovation in Kimi k2 is the adoption of the Muon optimizer (proposed by Keller Jordan et al., paper title: Muon: An Optimizer for Neural Networks with Orthogonalizing Momentum):

  • Core idea: Applies spectral orthogonalization to the momentum buffer
  • Advantage: Achieves stronger performance with fewer training steps compared to the traditional Adam optimizer
  • Significance: First successful application of a novel optimizer at the trillion-parameter model scale

Capability Positioning

  • Code generation: Strong performance on code benchmarks such as LiveCodeBench and SWE-bench
  • Mathematical reasoning: Competitive on math benchmarks such as MATH
  • Agent/tool use: Specifically designed for autonomous agent workflows, supporting complex tool calls and task orchestration
  • Open source: Released with open weights

4.3 Key Technology Trends

Trend 1: RL Becomes the Core Driver of Reasoning Capabilities

All three institutions independently adopted reinforcement learning as the key lever for enhancing reasoning capabilities:

  • DeepSeek demonstrated that pure RL can elicit reasoning capabilities (R1-Zero)
  • Qwen applied RL on pre-trained base models for significant performance gains (QwQ)
  • Kimi achieved long-chain reasoning through RL training and distilled it into short-chain models (k1.5)

Trend 2: MoE Architecture Becomes the Efficiency Benchmark

All three adopt or support MoE architectures:

  • Dramatically reduce inference costs through sparse activation
  • While maintaining or even surpassing dense model performance, keep active parameters in the 20–40B range
  • Make training and deployment of trillion-parameter models a reality

Trend 3: Fine-Grained Control of Reasoning Efficiency

Moving from “deep reasoning only” to “reasoning on demand”:

  • Qwen3’s /think / /no_think mode switching
  • Kimi k1.5’s Long2Short distillation
  • DeepSeek R1’s multi-tier distilled model series

Trend 4: Exponential Decline in Training Costs

  • DeepSeek-V3 trained a model comparable to GPT-4o for approximately $5.5 million
  • FP8 precision, GRPO, and other technical innovations drastically reduced compute requirements
  • MoE architecture further improved training efficiency

6. Conclusion

The research achievements of DeepSeek, Qwen, and Kimi between 2024 and 2025 collectively outline the frontier landscape of China’s large AI model field:

  1. DeepSeek excels in engineering efficiency and theoretical innovation — MLA, GRPO, and R1-Zero’s pure RL reasoning emergence each challenge existing paradigms
  2. Qwen wins through productization and ecosystem breadth — full-size coverage from 0.6B to 235B, Qwen3’s hybrid reasoning mode, and the largest open-source model matrix
  3. Kimi distinguishes itself through long context and training methodology innovation — Long2Short technology, trillion-parameter MoE training, and the engineering validation of the Muon optimizer

Together, these three organizations have accelerated several key trends: RL-driven reasoning, MoE architecture adoption, declining training costs, and fine-grained control of reasoning efficiency. These advances are not only significant at the technical level but also have profound implications for the global AI competitive landscape.
