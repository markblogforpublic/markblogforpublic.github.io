1. Company Overview

1.1 Founding Background and Mission

OpenAI was incorporated in December 2015 in Delaware, initially established as a non-profit artificial intelligence research organization. The founding team includes Sam Altman, Elon Musk, Ilya Sutskever, Greg Brockman, John Schulman, Andrej Karpathy, and other leading AI researchers and technology entrepreneurs. At its founding, OpenAI received approximately $1 billion in committed donations, with the mission statement of “ensuring that Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) benefits all of humanity.”

1.2 Organizational Structure Evolution

OpenAI’s organizational structure has undergone three major transformations:

  • 2015–2019: Pure non-profit phase. Operated as OpenAI Inc., focused on open AI research.
  • 2019–2024: Capped-Profit phase. In 2019, created OpenAI LP (a limited-profit subsidiary) and introduced Microsoft’s $1 billion investment. This transition sparked widespread controversy over whether OpenAI was straying from its original mission.
  • 2025–present: Public Benefit Corporation (PBC) phase. In 2025, completed a restructuring in which the subsidiary was converted into a Public Benefit Corporation, with the OpenAI Foundation holding 26% of shares. Microsoft had previously invested over $13 billion cumulatively.

2. Core Technologies and Models

2.1 GPT Series Evolution

GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) is OpenAI’s flagship product line, defining the development trajectory of large language models:

  • GPT-1 (2018): First demonstrated the viability of the “unsupervised pre-training + supervised fine-tuning” paradigm. 117 million parameters.
  • GPT-2 (2019): 1.5 billion parameters, demonstrated the text generation capabilities of large-scale language models. OpenAI temporarily refused to release the full model, citing it as “too dangerous.”
  • GPT-3 (2020): 175 billion parameters, first to demonstrate the powerful capabilities of “few-shot learning,” becoming the largest language model at the time. Opened to developers via API.
  • GPT-4 (2023.03): Multimodal model (accepting text and image input), achieving top-10% human performance on multiple professional examinations.
  • GPT-4o (2024.05): Omni model, natively supporting multimodal input/output across voice, text, and vision, with faster speed and lower cost.
  • GPT-4o mini (2024.07): Lightweight version targeting broader application scenarios.
  • GPT-4.5 (2025): Further expanded capabilities and efficiency.

2.2 Reasoning Model Series (o-series)

Starting in 2024, OpenAI pioneered the “reasoning model” product line:

  • o1 (2024.09): Introduced chain-of-thought reasoning, significantly surpassing GPT-4 on mathematics, coding, and scientific reasoning tasks.
  • o1 pro mode (2024.12): Enhanced reasoning mode, providing stronger reasoning capabilities for ChatGPT Pro users.
  • o3-mini (2025.01): A more efficient reasoning model.
  • o3 (announced 2024.12): Achieved breakthrough results on the ARC-AGI benchmark.

2.3 Other Important Models

  • DALL-E Series: Text-to-image models. DALL-E (2021), DALL-E 2 (2022.04), DALL-E 3 (2023), leading the wave of commercialization in AI image generation.
  • Whisper: Speech recognition model supporting multilingual transcription.
  • Sora (2024.02): Text-to-video model capable of generating high-quality videos up to one minute long, demonstrating cutting-edge AI capabilities in video understanding and generation.
  • Codex: Code generation model that directly gave rise to GitHub Copilot.

2.4 Product Ecosystem

  • ChatGPT: Launched in November 2022, it has become the most widely used AI conversational product globally, offering multiple tiers including Free, Plus, Team, and Enterprise.
  • GPT Store: Allows users to create and share custom GPT applications.
  • API platform: Provides comprehensive model API access for developers.
  • Operator (2025.01): An AI browser agent that can automatically execute web page tasks.

3.2 Key Technical Contributions

  • RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback): One of OpenAI’s most important technical contributions. It trains reward models using human preference data, then uses reinforcement learning to optimize language model outputs. The InstructGPT paper systematically demonstrated this method, which became the foundation for ChatGPT and all subsequent alignment techniques.
  • Scaling Laws: OpenAI’s 2020 research demonstrated that neural network performance follows predictable power-law relationships with model size, data volume, and compute. This discovery profoundly influenced model development strategies across the entire industry.
  • Chain-of-Thought Reasoning: A reasoning paradigm pioneered by the o-series models, enabling models to “think” before answering.

4. Policy and Governance

4.1 Evolution of Safety Policies

OpenAI was founded with “safe AGI development” as its core mission, but its safety stance has undergone significant changes amid commercialization:

  • Superalignment team: Established in July 2023, co-led by Ilya Sutskever and Jan Leike, with the goal of solving the alignment problem for superintelligent AI. It committed 20% of compute resources to this research. However, this team was effectively dissolved in May 2024.
  • Safety talent exodus: In 2024, approximately half of the in-house AI safety researchers departed. Jan Leike publicly criticized OpenAI for “putting shiny products above safety.” Ilya Sutskever also left in May 2024.
  • Military use policy changes: In 2024, it was revealed that OpenAI had quietly removed clauses from its usage policy that prohibited military and warfare applications.
  • Lobbying activities: OpenAI has faced criticism for lobbying against AI safety legislation.

4.2 Sam Altman Firing Incident (November 2023)

On November 17, 2023, OpenAI’s board abruptly fired CEO Sam Altman, citing “lack of confidence in his continued leadership.” Over the following five days:

  • Approximately 700 OpenAI employees (the vast majority of the workforce) signed a joint letter threatening collective resignation
  • Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella announced hiring Altman and Brockman
  • Ultimately, Altman was reinstated as CEO, and board members underwent major restructuring

This event deeply exposed the tensions between non-profit mission and commercial pressures in OpenAI’s governance structure.

4.3 Engagement with Government and Regulators

  • In May 2023, Sam Altman testified before the US Congress, calling for AI regulation.
  • In January 2025, participated in the Stargate project announced by the Trump administration, marking deep alignment with political power.
  • July 2025: The US DoD awarded OpenAI a $200 million one-year contract for military/national security AI tools. OpenAI subsequently launched the “OpenAI for Government” initiative, providing AI access to federal, state, and local governments.
  • February 28, 2026: OpenAI announced the deployment of its models to US DoD classified networks, marking a major escalation in military AI applications. This decision was widely reported by Politico, The Guardian, MIT Technology Review, New York Times, NBC News, Axios, and other media outlets.
  • Federal legislative lobbying: OpenAI has been actively advocating for federal AI legislation, with the intention of preempting federal legislation to supplant 781 existing state-level AI bills (as of March 2025, according to Oliver Roberts). This strategy has been criticized as an attempt to dilute the overall intensity of AI regulation through a unified federal framework.
  • Covert funding of children’s AI safety coalition: Wikipedia records show that OpenAI provided all initial funding to the “Parents & Kids Safe AI Coalition,” but multiple organizations contacted by the coalition stated they were unaware of OpenAI’s involvement. This conduct was described by multiple media outlets as “covert funding,” raising questions about the transparency of OpenAI’s lobbying strategies.

OpenAI’s aggressive expansion into government and military sectors stands in stark contrast to its founding mission of “benefiting all of humanity,” and underscores its complete transformation from a non-profit research institution into a commercial-governmental hybrid.

4.4 Non-Profit to For-Profit Conversion Controversy

  • In 2024–2025, OpenAI pursued conversion from a non-profit to a for-profit entity.
  • Elon Musk filed a lawsuit opposing this conversion, claiming it violated founding commitments.
  • In 2025, completed restructuring into a Public Benefit Corporation (PBC).

5.2 Revenue Model

OpenAI’s revenue comes primarily from three channels:

  1. ChatGPT subscription services: ChatGPT Plus ($20/month), ChatGPT Pro ($200/month), Team, and Enterprise tiers.
  2. API services: Model API calls for developers, billed by token.
  3. Enterprise solutions: ChatGPT Enterprise, customized services.

According to reports, OpenAI’s annualized revenue in 2024 was approximately $3.4 billion, projected to reach $11–12 billion+ in 2025, and expected to surpass $25 billion in 2026.

5.3 Stargate Project

In January 2025, OpenAI jointly announced the Stargate project with SoftBank, Oracle, and MGX (an Abu Dhabi fund), with total investment reaching up to $500 billion and an initial commitment of approximately $100 billion. The plan is to build hyperscale AI data center infrastructure in the United States. The project received public support from President Trump.

5.4 Compute Infrastructure: De-Nvidification Strategy

OpenAI is actively building independent compute infrastructure to reduce dependence on Nvidia:

DatePartnerDetails
2025.09OracleSigned a $300B five-year compute procurement contract, one of the largest single infrastructure deals in AI industry history
2025.10AMDSigned a multi-billion dollar agreement to procure 6GW of compute using MI450 chips, starting deployment from AMD’s MI450

Custom chip strategy: The custom AI chip being co-designed with Broadcom is a major strategic initiative aimed at optimizing both training and inference workloads while reducing dependence on Nvidia GPUs. The chip will be manufactured by TSMC using a 3nm process, with mass production targeted for 2026. It should be noted that Broadcom describes the chip as a “custom inference chip,” while OpenAI claims it supports “both training and inference,” with some discrepancy in the details. This strategy parallels other tech giants’ custom chip initiatives, such as Google’s TPU and Amazon’s Trainium.

Scale of the Oracle contract: The $300 billion five-year compute procurement contract is extremely rare in the AI industry. The source for this contract is Bloomberg News (via Wikipedia citation), and no independent SEC filings or official press releases have been found to confirm it. If the contract details are accurate, it would give OpenAI far greater compute reserves than its peers over the next five years.

  • Anthropic/Claude: Safety-oriented, supported by Amazon and Google
  • Meta/Llama: Open-source model approach
  • xAI/Grok: Under Elon Musk

6. Humanistic and Social Impact

6.1 Catalyzing the AI Boom

The launch of ChatGPT is widely considered the catalyst for the “AI boom.” It brought AI from research laboratories into the daily lives of ordinary consumers, triggering a global wave of AI investment, entrepreneurship, and application. Generative AI has had profound impacts across education, healthcare, law, creative industries, and other fields.

6.2 Ethics and Controversies

  • Copyright lawsuits: In 2023–2024, OpenAI faced copyright infringement lawsuits from authors, media companies, and others, alleging the use of copyrighted content to train models.
  • Data privacy: The sources and methods of training data usage have sparked ongoing controversy.
  • Employment impact: The widespread use of ChatGPT has triggered broad discussions about the prospects for white-collar employment.
  • Misinformation risks: Powerful text generation capabilities have amplified the risks of misinformation and deepfakes.

6.3 Talent Movement

OpenAI has been an important incubator for AI talent but has also experienced significant talent drain:

  • Andrej Karpathy (departed 2022, later joined other projects)
  • Ilya Sutskever (departed 2024, later founded Safe Superintelligence Inc.)
  • Jan Leike (departed 2024, joined Anthropic)
  • John Schulman (departed 2024, joined Anthropic)
  • Mira Murati (departed 2024, former CTO)

These departures have been widely interpreted as reflecting internal ideological divisions at OpenAI between safety and commercialization.

7. Future Outlook

7.1 AGI Vision

OpenAI has always positioned AGI as its ultimate goal. Sam Altman has stated multiple times that the company is committed to developing “AI systems smarter than humans.” In 2025, OpenAI announced plans to unify the GPT and o-series reasoning model lines, with GPT-5 expected to integrate the strengths of both product lines.

7.2 Compute Independence and Chip Autonomy

OpenAI is building compute infrastructure independent of Nvidia through three paths: Oracle, AMD, and Broadcom. The $300 billion Oracle contract, the 6GW chip deal with AMD, and the custom 3nm chip co-designed with Broadcom together constitute the most aggressive compute de-dependency strategy in the AI industry. If the custom chip achieves mass production in 2026, OpenAI will possess a complete AI technology stack from hardware to software.

7.3 Deep Integration with Government and Military AI

OpenAI has transformed from a non-profit research institution “benefiting all of humanity” into a commercial entity deeply embedded in the US defense system. The $200M DoD contract, classified network deployment, and federal lobbying to supplant 781 state-level AI bills all indicate that OpenAI is building competitive moats through government relationships. The covert funding of the children’s AI safety coalition reflects its multi-layered strategy for public influence.

7.4 Challenges

  • Balancing safety and capability: How to maintain safety commitments while rapidly advancing commercialization remains OpenAI’s most fundamental challenge.
  • Legal risks: Copyright lawsuits and organizational restructuring legal challenges persist.
  • Talent competition: How to retain top researchers amid intense talent wars.
  • Regulatory pressure: Global AI regulatory frameworks are taking shape, and OpenAI must adapt to a constantly evolving regulatory environment.
  • Compute investment returns: The $300 billion Oracle contract and $500 billion Stargate investment require massive revenue growth to support, and OpenAI must demonstrate that its business model can generate sufficient returns.
  • Militarization controversy: Deep involvement in defense AI may trigger public backlash and internal employee dissatisfaction, with the risk of a recurrence of the 2024 safety team exodus.

7.5 Opportunities

  • $500 billion infrastructure investment: The Stargate project will provide OpenAI with unprecedented compute resources.
  • Enterprise market penetration: Significant growth potential for ChatGPT Enterprise and API business.
  • Multimodal AI applications: Models like GPT-4o and Sora open doors to entirely new application scenarios.
  • AI agents: Products like Operator represent a paradigm shift from conversational AI to autonomous AI agents.
